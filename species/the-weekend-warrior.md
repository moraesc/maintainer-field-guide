# 🏋️ The Weekend Warrior

> *"Sorry for the delayed review — my day job doesn't know about my open source life."*

**Scientific Name:** *Contributorus weekendicus*
**Conservation Status:** Uncommon
**Habitat:** Pull requests, feature branches
**Threat Level to Maintainers:** 🟢 Harmless (beneficial)

---

## Description

The Weekend Warrior is the most beloved species in the open source ecosystem. Identified by their Saturday morning commit timestamps and meticulous PR descriptions, they represent the backbone of volunteer open source contribution.

During the workweek, the Weekend Warrior is indistinguishable from any other developer — writing code for an employer, attending standups, pretending to enjoy sprint retrospectives. But when Friday evening arrives, a transformation occurs. They crack open their laptop, brew a cup of coffee, and dive into the codebase they actually care about.

Their contributions are consistently high-quality, well-tested, and appropriately scoped. They read the CONTRIBUTING.md before submitting. They respond to review feedback promptly (on Saturday). They are, in short, the species every maintainer wishes they could clone.

---

## Behavioral Patterns

- Commits cluster on Saturday and Sunday mornings
- PR descriptions are thorough and reference related issues
- Always runs tests locally before pushing
- Responds to code review within one weekend cycle
- Gradually earns trust and eventually becomes a core contributor
- Has a "day job" that has no idea they maintain critical infrastructure on weekends
