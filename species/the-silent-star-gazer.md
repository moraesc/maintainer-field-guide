## Description

The Silent Star-Gazer is the most numerous species in the open source ecosystem, outnumbering all other species combined by a factor of approximately 100:1. They are identified by a single behavior: pressing the ⭐ button. Then they leave.

No issues. No PRs. No discussions. No forks. Just a star, and silence.

Despite contributing zero code, the Silent Star-Gazer plays a vital ecological role. Their stars provide the social proof that attracts other species to the repository. A repo with 10,000 stars attracts Weekend Warriors. A repo with 10 stars attracts tumbleweeds.

Some maintainers have attempted to communicate with Silent Star-Gazers by adding "If you use this project, please consider contributing!" to their README. Studies show this has a conversion rate of approximately 0.003%.

## Behavioral Patterns

- Stars the repo and immediately leaves
- Never returns to check for updates
- Has starred 847 repositories
- Has contributed to zero of them
- Occasionally unstars during controversy, then re-stars a week later
- May also Watch the repo, generating notification guilt
