# 🔤 The Drive-By Typo Fixer

> *"I was just passing through and noticed you spelled 'recieve' wrong in your README."*

**Scientific Name:** *Correctorus drivebius*
**Conservation Status:** Abundant
**Habitat:** README files, documentation, code comments
**Threat Level to Maintainers:** 🟢 Harmless

---

## Description

The Drive-By Typo Fixer is perhaps the most commonly observed species in the open source ecosystem. Identifiable by their single-commit pull requests and surgically precise changes, they swoop in, fix a spelling error, and vanish — never to be seen again.

While individually harmless, large migrations of Drive-By Typo Fixers can overwhelm maintainer notification inboxes, particularly during Hacktoberfest season. Their PRs are almost always mergeable, but the cognitive cost of reviewing dozens of one-character changes adds up.

Some researchers believe the Drive-By Typo Fixer is actually a larval form of the Weekend Warrior, in the early stages of metamorphosis before evolving into a full contributor.

---

## Behavioral Patterns

- Opens exactly one PR, fixing exactly one typo
- Never opens an issue first
- PR description is either empty or says "Fixed typo"
- Disappears immediately after opening the PR
- Has a mass migration event during Hacktoberfest
- Occasionally fixes a "typo" that was actually intentional (e.g., British spelling)
